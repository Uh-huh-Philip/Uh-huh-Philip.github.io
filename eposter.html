Introduction

Cloud computing has been embraced by many enterprises as one of the most emerging IT technology which is believed to have the potential to transform the the way IT products and services are designed and distributed (2010, Armbrust, Michael, et al.) and reduces the upfront costs for IT construction in enterprises (2011, Marston, Sean, et al). Meanwhile, the industry has never stop exploring to improve the performance of cloud computing. A new technology called Container has been introduced as a more efficient application deployment paradigm compared to traditional hypervisor-based deployment.

Research Methods
Literature review: All research should be conducted with awareness of existing knowledge in relevant subject areas(2004, Rowley and Slack). Case study: A case study is a research method involving multiple data collection methods to gather information from natural individuals, legal entities or public database in a non-experimental context(1987, Benbasat, Goldstein and Mead). For this research, researcher would collect data from both vendors and clients. Representative from a cloud compute provider would be review to learn as many container technology application cases as possible. Cloud computing
The NIST Definition of Cloud Computing

Cloud computing is a model for enabling ubiquitous, convenient, on-demand network access to a shared pool of configurable computing resources (e.g., networks, servers, storage, applications, and services) that can be rapidly provisioned and released with minimal management effort or service provider interaction.

Containers
Pokémon powered by containers

Beyond being a global phenomenon, Pokémon GO is one of the most exciting examples of container-based development in the wild. The application logic for the game runs on Google Container Engine (GKE) powered by the open source Kubernetes project. Niantic chose GKE for its ability to orchestrate their container cluster at planetary-scale, freeing its team to focus on deploying live changes for their players. In this way, Niantic used Google Cloud to turn Pokémon GO into a service for millions of players, continuously adapting and improving.
